## 1. Diagnostic (Phase 1) — DONE

The species-disjoint generalisation gap is **clade-driven**, not uniform.

| SF (epoch 8)        | macro recall | worst-2-species share of misses | clade pattern                                  |
|---------------------|-------------:|--------------------------------:|------------------------------------------------|
| DNA/hAT             | 0.38         | **89 %** (664/745)              | aAnoBae R=0.055; aPelLes R=0.270 (amphib./turtle) |
| DNA/PiggyBac        | 0.58         | **100 %** (55/55)               | kTriCli, aPelLes                               |
| DNA/PIF-Harbinger   | 0.54         | 80 % (61/76)                    | aAnoBae, aPelLes                               |
| DNA/CMC             | 0.50         | 78 % (7/9)                      | kTriCli                                        |
| DNA/TcMar-Tc1       | 0.82         | 67 % (35/52)                    | rThaEle, kTriCli                               |
| DNA (unspec.)       | 0.71         | 39 % (24/62)                    | uniform                                        |

Epoch 30 (best-by-val) is **strictly worse on every clade-driven SF**:
hAT recall on aAnoBae 0.055 → 0.009, aPelLes 0.270 → 0.057. Strong
evidence that prolonged training memorises train-species hAT and forgets
amphibian/reptile hAT.

→ The seed-42 outer split sent four amphibians + a turtle to test with no
phylogenetic neighbour in train. **Strategy 5 (phylo-rebalanced sampling)
is therefore promoted from ★★ to ★★★★** in `cross_species_strategies.md`.
Strategy 1 (augmentation) remains ★★★★★ as a safe default.

## 2. Smoke test — strategy 1 (augmentation) — DONE

`run_smoke_aug.py --both` on MPS, EPOCHS=3, SUBSET_SIZE=5000.

| arm                | cls macro F1 | SF macro F1 | gate (cnn, gnn) |
|--------------------|-------------:|------------:|-----------------|
| aug                | 0.832        | 0.041       | 0.19, 0.81      |
| no-aug             | 0.801        | 0.058       | 0.17, 0.83      |
| **Δ (aug − noaug)**| **+0.031**   | **−0.016**  | —               |

**Acceptance criterion (Δ SF macro F1 ≥ +0.02): NOT met.**

Caveats: at 5000 samples × 3 epochs both arms severely underfit the 8-way
SF head (5/8 superfamilies score F1 = 0.000). The signal is dominated by
cls vs SF imbalance and tiny per-SF support in the held-out test
(n_hAT = 192, n_TcMar = 46). The smoke is *not* powered to detect the
+0.02 effect we need; both arms are below the regime in which augmentation
helps.

## 3. Recommendation for full-scale follow-up

Given the diagnostic is **concentrated** (not uniform) and the smoke is
negative-but-uninformative:

1. **Run the full 25-epoch strategy-1 + strategy-5 stack** on CSD3
   (slurm script in `models/slurm_submit_hybrid_v5.sh`). Use the full
   train set with `WeightedRandomSampler` weighted by inverse
   (clade × SF) frequency. This addresses the dominant failure mode
   (no clade-relative for aPelLes-style hAT in train) directly.
2. **Stop at epoch 8–10** (early-stopping by val). Epoch 30 demonstrably
   forgets clade-disjoint SFs.
3. If 1+5 raises SF macro F1 by ≥0.05 → ship. Otherwise add **strategy 3
   (DANN species-adversarial)** as the next experiment; do not move to
   prototypical heads (strategy 4) until features are de-biased.

The augmentation hyperparameters in `run_smoke_aug.py` (`P_RC=0.5,
P_MUT=0.005, P_WINDOW_DROP_RATE=0.05`) were not invalidated by the
smoke — they should be re-tested at full scale rather than retuned blind.
